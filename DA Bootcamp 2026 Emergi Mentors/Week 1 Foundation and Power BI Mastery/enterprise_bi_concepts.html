🧠 Critical Concepts to Master

These foundational concepts are essential for understanding and implementing enterprise BI solutions.

1. Star Schema

Fact table in center (transactions) connected to dimension tables (context) in a star pattern.

Why it matters: Fastest query performance. Business users understand it intuitively. Industry standard for data warehousing.

FactSales (center) ├─ DimDate ├─ DimProduct ├─ DimCustomer └─ DimRegion

2. Fact vs Dimension

Facts = Measurements (numbers, metrics). Dimensions = Context (who, what, where, when).

Why it matters: Determines table structure and model design. Facts aggregate, dimensions filter.

Fact: Revenue, Quantity, Cost Dimension: Product Name, Region, Date

3. Data Grain

What one row represents. Most important modeling decision.

Why it matters: Wrong grain = wrong analysis. Must be clearly defined before building model.

FactSales grain: 1 row = 1 transaction FactPlanning grain: 1 row = 1 product-month

4. One-to-Many Relationships

One dimension row relates to many fact rows. Always 1:* cardinality.

Why it matters: Foundation of star schema. Enables filtering from dimensions to facts.

DimProduct[ProductID] (1) ↓ FactSales[ProductID] (*) One product → many sales

5. Filter Direction

Filters flow from dimensions to facts. Single direction only.

Why it matters: Prevents circular logic. Ensures predictable query results. Performance optimization.

User selects "NSW" in Region ↓ DimRegion filters ↓ FactSales shows NSW only

6. ETL Process

Extract data from sources, Transform it (clean, calculate), Load to model.

Why it matters: Data quality starts here. Automated ETL eliminates manual work.

Extract: CSV, Excel, Database Transform: Power Query (M) Load: Power BI Model

7. DAX Measures

Calculations that respond to filter context. Computed at query time, not stored.

Why it matters: Enable dynamic KPIs. More flexible and performant than calculated columns.

Total Revenue = SUM(FactSales[Revenue]) Gross Margin % = DIVIDE([Profit], [Revenue])

8. Row-Level Security

Filter data based on who is viewing it. Same report, different data per user.

Why it matters: Data governance and compliance. Regional managers see only their territory.

Role: NSW Manager Filter: [Region] = "NSW" Result: User only sees NSW data

9. Conformed Dimensions

Same dimension used by multiple fact tables. Shared across business processes.

Why it matters: Enables cross-functional analysis. "Which products have high marketing spend but low sales?"

DimProduct used by: - FactSales - FactMarketingSpend - FactPlanning

10. Time Intelligence

Built-in DAX functions for year-over-year, YTD, running totals. Requires marked date table.

Why it matters: Business users need trend analysis and comparisons to prior periods.

Revenue PY = CALCULATE([Revenue], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(Date)) Revenue YTD = TOTALYTD([Revenue], Date)

🎓 Key Takeaways

  • Star schema is the foundation - Facts in center, dimensions around them
  • Grain definition is the most critical modeling decision
  • One-to-many relationships flow from dimensions to facts (single direction)
  • Measures are more flexible than calculated columns - default to measures
  • Mark your date table to unlock time intelligence functions
  • Row-level security enables governed self-service analytics
  • ETL automation reduces manual reporting time by 80%+
  • Conformed dimensions enable cross-functional analysis
  • This pattern scales from 100K rows to 100M rows
